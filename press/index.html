UHR

an intensely awkwark three hander

Art-punk three-piece Uhr formed in the halcyon months just prior to the arrival of Covid-19 and the first lockdown back in 2020. This put the band on hold for a year or so, until rehearsal space opened up again, and they could get back to work. All three members have previous musically, with Jack and Dave most recently being part of The Common Cold, who released the acclaimed album "Shut Up! Yo Liberals" in 2018. Uhr debuted at the Preston Popfest event in August 2021, sharing the bill with the likes of The Wolfhounds, Stuart Moxham, Jazz Butcher, The Great Leap Forward and more. Comparisons were made to Gang of Four, Wire, and late 80s Glaswegian pop-noise outfit Dawson. Taut rhythms, cheese wire guitar riffs and oblique lyrics garnered an enthusiastic reception from the tired weekend crowd. Hooking up with Own It Records (Girls In Synthesis), February 2022 sees the release of two tracks, Eskimo and Written Reply.

Jack Harkins - a visual artist specialising in environmental installation who is studying fine art at Manchester School of Art. As a musician Jack plays and writes with uhr (bass & vocals) and blanketman (guitar). Jack was a founder member of Preston bands Ludovico & Ginnel and toured with The Common Cold.
John Harkins - guitarist, noise box creator & producer. John played guitar in 80's shamblers Things in General. More recently John has played with and produced Indie-Pop duo Crossbill and folk-rock band Deadwood Dog. John has a number of side projects most notably The Crufts
Dave Chambers - started drumming with Cornershop in the late 80's and continued on through the 90's with Formula One and The Wandering Step. After a few years out of action Dave returned to the fray with The Common Cold and then Uhr.
